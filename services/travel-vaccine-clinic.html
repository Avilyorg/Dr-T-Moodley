Travel & Paediatric
Vaccine Clinic

Travel plans are exciting, and school milestones matter. In both cases, your health sits quietly in the background, shaping what is possible. Our travel and paediatric vaccine clinic in Tongaat and Umhlanga helps you prepare properly so that you can move forward with confidence.

Why are vaccines important for my child?

Childhood immunisations protect against serious illnesses that can disrupt their growth, schooling and everyday life. Vaccination is not only about today, but it is also about long-term protection during critical stages of development.

At our clinic, your child's vaccination record is carefully reviewed. If doses have been missed, a safe catch-up plan can be arranged without restarting the schedule. You receive clear guidance on timing, possible side effects and what to expect afterwards.

  • Routine childhood immunisations according to the South African schedule
  • School-entry vaccine updates
  • Catch-up vaccination planning

If your little one lives with a chronic condition such as diabetes or requires HIV care, vaccine timing is coordinated with their broader management plan to ensure stability and safety.

Do I need a vaccine to travel abroad?

Different destinations carry different health risks. Some countries require proof of vaccination for entry. Others strongly recommend protection against region-specific infections.

Before departure, you can expect the following discussion points at our clinic:

  • Your travel destination and duration
  • The type of travel, whether business, leisure or adventure-based
  • Your current health status and medical history

Recommended travel vaccines are then explained clearly. You receive documentation where required, along with practical advice about food safety, insect precautions and managing existing medical conditions while abroad.

If you are preparing for long-haul flights or aviation-related work, discussions can also align with your broader aviation medical requirements, ensuring you remain fit for both travel and flight.

What if I have underlying health concerns?

Travel health does not exist in isolation. If you are managing hypertension, obesity or another long-term condition, this is taken into account before vaccination. Medical schedules are reviewed, and adjustments can be discussed where necessary.

For corporate travellers requiring executive medical assessments, vaccination planning can be integrated into your broader medical evaluation. The goal is clarity, and no surprises or last-minute stress.

What can I expect on the day?

Appointments are structured and efficient. You receive a clear explanation before any vaccine is administered. Aftercare advice is provided in plain language. Children are approached calmly, with time allowed for questions and reassurance.

You leave knowing what has been given, what comes next and when to return if follow-up doses are required.

Your health journey does not pause for travel or childhood milestones, but continues. If you need routine family care, support with weight management, or guidance for an upcoming trip, you can address it in one setting.
