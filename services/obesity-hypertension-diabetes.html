Centre for Obesity,
Hypertension & Diabetes Excellence

Obesity, hypertension and diabetes rarely arrive alone. They develop together, reinforce one another, and together they raise cardiovascular risk significantly. Managing each in isolation misses the full picture. Our Centre for Obesity, Hypertension and Diabetes Excellence in Tongaat and Umhlanga takes an integrated approach — treating these conditions as the interconnected challenges they are, with care that is structured, evidence-based and built around your specific circumstances.

Why address these three conditions together?

Obesity increases insulin resistance, which drives blood sugar upward. Elevated blood sugar strains the cardiovascular system, raising blood pressure. High blood pressure then accelerates complications from both diabetes and excess weight. The cycle is well established, and breaking it requires looking at all three simultaneously.

Treating blood pressure without addressing weight, or managing glucose without examining diet and activity, produces limited results. This centre exists because coordinated care produces better outcomes.

What does an assessment involve?

Your initial consultation is thorough. We begin by building a complete picture of your current health status.

This includes a review of:

  • Body composition, weight history and BMI
  • Blood pressure readings and cardiovascular risk profile
  • Fasting glucose, HbA1c and metabolic markers
  • Medication history and adherence
  • Lifestyle habits including diet, activity level and sleep

Results are interpreted in plain language. You understand what each number means, how it relates to the others, and what steps will address the findings.

How is ongoing care structured?

Each follow-up appointment tracks your progress against the targets we set together. Blood pressure, glucose control and weight trends are reviewed at every visit.

Medication adjustments are made where needed. Dietary guidance is revisited as your weight shifts and metabolic markers respond. The plan is not static — it evolves as you improve.

If structured weight management or sports nutrition support is appropriate, this can be incorporated into your overall plan to accelerate and sustain progress.

What lifestyle changes make the biggest difference?

Medication alone cannot reverse the trajectory of these conditions. Sustainable lifestyle change is the foundation on which medical treatment works.

You receive practical, achievable guidance on:

  • Nutritional choices that reduce glycaemic load and support healthy weight
  • Physical activity appropriate to your current fitness and any joint or cardiovascular limitations
  • Sleep and stress management, which directly affect insulin sensitivity and blood pressure

Unrealistic targets are counterproductive. Our approach focuses on steady, measurable improvement rather than dramatic short-term measures that cannot be maintained.

When should complications be considered?

Long-standing hypertension and diabetes can affect the kidneys, eyes and peripheral nerves. These complications develop gradually and are often silent in early stages.

Regular monitoring through your chronic condition reviews helps detect early changes. Where further assessment is needed, referrals are arranged promptly.

For individuals in high-responsibility roles who require executive or insurance medicals, or those undergoing aviation medical examinations, stable control of these conditions is essential. Poorly managed hypertension or diabetes can affect certification outcomes. Early, consistent care protects both your health and your professional standing.

Is this service right for me?

If you have been told you have pre-diabetes, hypertension or are carrying excess weight, this centre is designed for you. You do not need to have a formal diagnosis of all three conditions to benefit. Early intervention is the most effective point of care.

If you are already managing one or more of these conditions and feel your current approach is not producing steady results, a structured review can identify what is missing and recalibrate your plan.
